# Master Direction on Cyber Resilience and Digital Security Controls — Version 2 (Revised: July 2026)
# Domain: CYBERSECURITY
# Source: RBI/2026-27/121 DoS.CO.CSITEG/SEC.9/31.01.015/2026-27
# Issued: July 2, 2026
# Addressed to: All Scheduled Commercial Banks and Small Finance Banks
# Note: Issued in supersession of circular RBI/2025-26/88 DoS.CO.CSITEG/SEC.4/31.01.015/2025-26 dated October 15, 2025.

## Paragraph 12 - Governance and Board Oversight
The Board of Directors shall approve a cyber security policy annually and review its implementation at least once every quarter. Banks shall designate a Chief Information Security Officer who shall report directly to the Managing Director and shall not hold any concurrent operational technology role. The CISO shall present a consolidated cyber risk report to the Board's IT Strategy Committee at every meeting. Responsibility for cyber resilience shall rest with the Board and shall not be delegated to any external service provider.

## Paragraph 18 - Cyber Incident Reporting
Banks shall report all cyber security incidents to the Reserve Bank of India within two hours of detection. A detailed root cause analysis report must be submitted to the Department of Supervision within five working days of the incident. Banks shall also notify CERT-In in accordance with the directions issued under Section 70B of the Information Technology Act, 2000. Incidents affecting customer data shall additionally be disclosed to affected customers within twenty four hours. Failure to report incidents within the prescribed timelines shall attract supervisory action.

## Paragraph 24 - Multi-Factor Authentication
Banks shall implement multi-factor authentication for all internet-facing applications used by customers and employees. Risk-based authentication shall not be used as a substitute for a second factor for any transaction irrespective of value. Administrative access to critical systems must be protected by hardware-based authentication tokens. Authentication logs shall be retained for a minimum period of two years.

## Paragraph 30 - Vulnerability Assessment and Penetration Testing
Banks shall conduct vulnerability assessments of all critical information systems at least once every quarter. Penetration testing of internet-facing systems shall be carried out at least once every year by CERT-In empanelled auditors. All critical and high severity vulnerabilities must be remediated within fifteen days of identification. A summary of open vulnerabilities and remediation status shall be placed before the IT Strategy Committee every quarter.

## Paragraph 42 - Zero Trust Architecture Adoption
Banks shall adopt a zero trust architecture for all critical information systems and are required to complete implementation within twelve months from the date of this circular. Every access request must be authenticated, authorized and encrypted regardless of network origin, and implicit trust based on network location is prohibited. Banks shall not permit any unmanaged device to connect to systems processing customer data. A board-approved zero trust implementation roadmap shall be submitted to the Department of Supervision within ninety days of the date of this circular. Non-compliance with these directions shall attract penal action under Section 47A of the Banking Regulation Act, 1949.
