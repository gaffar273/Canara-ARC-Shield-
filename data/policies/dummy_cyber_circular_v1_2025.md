# Master Direction on Cyber Resilience and Digital Security Controls — Version 1 (Baseline: October 2025)
# Domain: CYBERSECURITY
# Source: RBI/2025-26/88 DoS.CO.CSITEG/SEC.4/31.01.015/2025-26
# Issued: October 15, 2025
# Addressed to: All Scheduled Commercial Banks and Small Finance Banks

## Paragraph 12 - Governance and Board Oversight
The Board of Directors shall approve a cyber security policy annually and review its implementation at least once every quarter. Banks shall designate a Chief Information Security Officer who shall report directly to the Managing Director and shall not hold any concurrent operational technology role. The CISO shall present a consolidated cyber risk report to the Board's IT Strategy Committee at every meeting. Responsibility for cyber resilience shall rest with the Board and shall not be delegated to any external service provider.

## Paragraph 18 - Cyber Incident Reporting
Banks shall report all cyber security incidents to the Reserve Bank of India within six hours of detection. A detailed root cause analysis report must be submitted to the Department of Supervision within seven working days of the incident. Banks shall also notify CERT-In in accordance with the directions issued under Section 70B of the Information Technology Act, 2000. Incidents affecting customer data shall additionally be disclosed to affected customers within seventy two hours. Failure to report incidents within the prescribed timelines shall attract supervisory action.

## Paragraph 24 - Multi-Factor Authentication
Banks shall implement multi-factor authentication for all internet-facing applications used by customers and employees. For low-value transactions below five thousand rupees, banks may adopt risk-based authentication in place of a second factor. Administrative access to critical systems must be protected by hardware-based authentication tokens. Authentication logs shall be retained for a minimum period of one year.

## Paragraph 30 - Vulnerability Assessment and Penetration Testing
Banks shall conduct vulnerability assessments of all critical information systems at least once every quarter. Penetration testing of internet-facing systems shall be carried out at least once every year by CERT-In empanelled auditors. All critical and high severity vulnerabilities must be remediated within fifteen days of identification. A summary of open vulnerabilities and remediation status shall be placed before the IT Strategy Committee every quarter.

## Paragraph 36 - Legacy System Isolation
Banks operating legacy systems that no longer receive security patches from the vendor shall isolate such systems in a segregated network zone. Compensating controls including enhanced monitoring and restricted access shall be documented for each legacy system. Banks shall prepare a decommissioning roadmap for all legacy systems and submit it to the Department of Supervision by March 31, 2026. No new business process shall be built on an unsupported legacy platform.
